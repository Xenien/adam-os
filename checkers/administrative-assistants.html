Administrative openings routinely pull in hundreds of applications, which is exactly why software makes the first cut. Over 90% of large employers screen applications with an applicant tracking system, and roughly 75% of resumes are cut before a human reads them. 3 resume tips for administrative assistants

  1. Break "office support" into its named parts. "Supported the executive team" matches nothing. "Managed calendars for 3 directors, coordinated domestic and international travel, and processed monthly expense reports" matches three terms postings actually screen for — and it's the same work, described precisely.
  2. List your software by name, including the boring ones. Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Google Workspace, Zoom, Slack. Admin postings screen on tools more than almost any other field, and "proficient in office software" matches none of them. A plain Skills list the parser can read is the fix.
  3. Quantify the volume you handled. Calls fielded per day, executives supported, meetings scheduled per week, invoices processed per month, events coordinated per year. "Scheduled 40+ meetings a week across 4 time zones" survives the software and tells the human reader you can handle their load.
