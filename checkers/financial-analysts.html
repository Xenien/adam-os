3 resume tips for financial analysts

  1. Name the analysis, not just the deliverable. "Prepared monthly board reporting" is a deliverable; "built driver-based financial models and monthly variance analysis against budget" names two disciplines postings screen for. Describe each deliverable by the method behind it.
  2. Spell out your credentials and systems both ways. "Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA)", "FP&A", "SAP", "GAAP" — certifications and systems are often hard filters, different tools search different forms, and a credential buried mid-sentence is easy for a parser to miss. Give them their own lines.
  3. Attach dollar figures and deltas to your bullets. Budget size owned, forecast accuracy improvement, cost variances identified, hours cut from the close. "Automated the monthly close package in Excel and Power BI, cutting reporting time from 5 days to 2" is what both the software and the hiring manager reward.
