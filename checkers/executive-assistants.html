3 resume tips for executive assistants

  1. Say who you supported and at what level. "Executive Assistant to the CEO and CFO of a 400-person company" ranks and reads differently from "administrative support." Seniority of your principals, number of executives, and company size are the context every EA posting is trying to establish.
  2. Turn "handled everything" into the posting's nouns. Complex calendar management across time zones, international travel coordination, board meeting preparation, expense reports, event planning, vendor management. Each function you name in the posting's own words is a keyword match; "wore many hats" matches nothing.
  3. Quantify the trust and the volume. Executives supported, meetings scheduled per week, board decks prepared per quarter, budgets or vendor contracts managed, events delivered and at what headcount. "Coordinated quarterly board meetings and offsites for 60+ attendees, on budget for 3 years running" survives the screen and signals reliability — the thing EA hiring actually turns on.
