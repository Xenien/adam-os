3 resume tips for electricians

  1. Put your license and safety credentials on their own lines, spelled out. "Journeyman Electrician — [your state] License #", "OSHA 30-Hour Construction", "First Aid/CPR certified." Licenses are the hardest filter in trade hiring — a parser that can't find yours may drop you regardless of experience.
  2. Name the settings and systems, not just the trade. Residential, commercial, or industrial; new construction vs. service work; the specific competencies the posting lists — blueprint reading, conduit and electrical wiring, preventive maintenance, building codes and permitting. Match the posting's own words for the work you've genuinely done.
  3. Quantify the jobs. Square footage wired, units completed, crew size supervised, inspection pass rate, years without a recordable incident. "Wired 60+ residential units a year, passing inspection first time on 95%" reads as proof — to the software's ranking and to the human who calls you back.
