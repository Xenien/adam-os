3 resume tips for data analysts

  1. Name the stack behind every deliverable. Not "built dashboards" but "built Tableau dashboards on a Snowflake warehouse, modeled with dbt." Screening software searches for the product names; a stackless bullet is invisible to it.
  2. Keep SQL and Excel on the resume even when they feel too obvious. They're the two most screened-for analyst terms, and analysts routinely drop them to sound more senior — then fail the keyword match on the basics.
  3. Quantify the decision, not just the analysis. Rows processed matter less than "identified churn drivers that informed a retention push, reducing churn 12%." Numbers tied to business outcomes survive screening and convince the hiring manager who reads you next.
