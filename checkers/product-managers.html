3 resume tips for product managers

  1. Show the craft behind each win. "Doubled activation" is an outcome; "doubled activation by running 14 A/B tests against a quarterly roadmap built from user research" is an outcome plus three keyword matches. For every result, name the method that produced it in the posting's own vocabulary.
  2. Keep a plain Skills line for tools and methods. Jira, Confluence, Figma, SQL, Google Analytics; Agile, Scrum, OKRs. PMs often skip the skills section as beneath the role — but it's the easiest place for both the parser and a searching recruiter to confirm the basics.
  3. Anchor every bullet in a metric that mattered. Adoption, retention, conversion, revenue, NPS, cycle time — with the size of the change and the user base. "Shipped a redesigned onboarding flow to 200K monthly users, lifting week-1 retention 9 points" is the sentence format PM screens and hiring managers both reward.
