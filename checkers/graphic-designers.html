3 resume tips for graphic designers

  1. Keep the resume itself parser-plain. One column, standard headings, real text — no skill bars, no icon grids, no text inside images. Save the visual craft for the portfolio, and put the portfolio URL in plain text near your contact info so both the parser and the recruiter can grab it.
  2. Name every tool, even the ones you think are implied. "Adobe Creative Suite" alone isn't enough when the posting says Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, or After Effects — recruiters search the specific application. List the suite and the individual tools you actually use, plus Figma if you touch product work.
  3. Attach outcomes to the craft. Design bullets drift into pure description ("designed social assets"). Add the scale and the result: "designed 30+ campaign assets a month across web, email, and social; rebrand lifted email click-through 22%." Numbers are what make a creative resume rank — and get believed.
