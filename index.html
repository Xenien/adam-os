      An ATS won't reject you. It just won't find you.
      
    
      Here's the truth the "beat the robots" ads won't tell you: most applicant tracking systems don't
    auto-reject anyone. What actually happens is quieter and crueler — a recruiter searches a database of
    hundreds of applicants by keyword, and if your resume parsed badly or says "managed projects" where
    they searched "project management," you're on page nine. Not rejected. Never found.
      
    
      That's why the rejections and silence piling up in your inbox aren't a verdict on your experience
    or your worth. Hard-working people spend hours polishing resumes and still lose — not to a stronger
    candidate, but to a wording mismatch and a parser that mangled their two-column layout.
      
    
      JobFit checks the two things that are actually real: does the machine read your
    file correctly, and will a recruiter's search find you. The full diagnosis is free — every keyword

      FAQ
      
    
      Is my resume really private?
      Yes. Open your browser's network tab and run a scan — you'll see zero requests. The entire analysis is JavaScript running on your device. We physically cannot see your resume.
      
    
      How is the score calculated?
      We extract the skills, tools, and repeated terms the job description emphasizes, weight them by importance, and measure how many appear in your resume (80% of the score). The remaining 20% comes from ATS formatting checks like contact info, standard section headings, and quantified achievements.
      
    
      Why keywords? Isn't that gaming the system?
      Applicant tracking systems and recruiters both search by keywords. If you have the skill but your resume says it differently than the posting does, you lose. JobFit only recommends adding keywords that are true of you — it's translation, not fabrication.
