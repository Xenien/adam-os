# JobFit — The $100M-Framework Marketing Plan

This plan runs the business through the Hormozi frameworks: **$100M Offers**
(value equation, Grand Slam Offer), **$100M Leads** (lead magnets, the Core Four,
paid ads), and **$100M Money Models** (attraction → core → premium, client-financed
acquisition). Each section states the framework, the audit finding, and what was done.

---

## 1. The Value Equation (Offers)

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

| Lever | Before | Now |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream outcome** | "Check your resume" (nobody's dream) | **Interviews.** Hero: "You didn't get rejected. You got filtered." Pricing header: "Get more interviews, or it's free." |
| **Perceived likelihood** | None | 30-day **Interview Guarantee** (refund + keep bonuses), the 75%-filtered stat, live free scan as proof-before-purchase |
| **Time delay** | Instant (good) | Kept. "In 10 seconds" is in the copy everywhere. |
| **Effort & sacrifice** | Paste-only, DIY | PDF upload (drag & drop), copy-paste rewrite patterns, copy-button templates — moved from "do it yourself" toward "done with you" |

## 2. Grand Slam Offer construction

**Stack** (Pro, $29 one-time — "The Interview Engine"):
1. Unlimited keyword-match reports — *the thing Jobscan rents at $49.95/mo*
2. All 8 ATS checks with exact fixes
3. Bullet rewrite patterns (fill-in-the-number templates)
4. Print-ready PDF report per application
5. Bonus: recruiter outreach scripts (4 templates)
6. Bonus: follow-up email templates (2)
7. Bonus: LinkedIn ATS checklist (10 points)

**Risk reversal:** Interview Guarantee — 5 real applications, 30 days, full refund,
keep the bonuses. (Refund-plus-keep is the Hormozi "better than risk-free" pattern;
digital COGS ≈ $0 so a refund costs nothing but the fee.)

**Scarcity/urgency (honest):** "Launch price $29 — rises to $49 after the first 100
licenses." ⚠ **Owner obligation: actually raise the price at 100 sales.** Fake
scarcity destroys the brand and violates FTC rules. Gumroad shows the sales count.

**Price:** $9 → **$29** (anchored to crossed-out $49 and Jobscan's $50/mo).
Hormozi: in a vacuum people equate price with value; a $9 tool reads as a toy.
$29 stays an impulse buy while tripling revenue per sale. Coach: $39 → **$99**,
anchored against the $150–$700 coaches charge per resume.

**Naming (M-A-G-I-C):** offer renamed "Pro — The Interview Engine" (avatar: job
seeker; goal: interviews; container: engine). Coach tier: "Commercial license."

## 3. Money Model (attraction → core → premium)

```
FREE scan (attraction offer, on-site, instant gratification)
  └─ FREE "2026 ATS Keyword Cheat Sheet" — $0 Gumroad product ⇒ EMAIL CAPTURED
       └─ CORE: Pro $29 (launch) → $49
            └─ PREMIUM: Coach $99 (same checkout, variant pick — a price-anchor
               that also makes $29 feel small)
```

- **Lead capture with zero infrastructure:** the cheat sheet is a $0+ Gumroad
  product. Every downloader becomes an email on your Gumroad list. Gumroad's
  built-in email feature ("Emails") then sends the follow-up sequence.
- **Follow-up sequence** (write once in Gumroad, runs forever):
  - Day 0: cheat sheet delivery + "run your free scan" link
  - Day 2: the 75%-filtered story + one tip from the checks + scan link
  - Day 5: case-style walkthrough (score 34 → 86) + Pro pitch + guarantee
  - Day 9: "launch price ends at 100 licenses" reminder (only while true)
- **Downsell:** in Gumroad, offer a 20% discount code on checkout abandonment
  (Gumroad has native abandoned-cart emails on paid plans; otherwise the Day-9
  email carries a REVIVE20 code).
- **Continuity (later):** a $5/mo "fresh keyword data" tier is possible but NOT
  worth it pre-traction. Money Models order: get the core offer converting first.

## 4. Client-Financed Acquisition math (the ads gate)

Hormozi's rule: a customer's first-30-day gross profit should cover **at least 2×**
the cost to acquire them — then ad spend is self-funding and scale is unlimited.

- Price $29 − Gumroad ~13% ≈ **$25 gross profit** (COGS ≈ $0)
- **Target CAC ≤ $12.50** (2× covered) · **Hard ceiling $25** (breakeven)
- Coach sales ($99 → ~$86 GP) are the margin cushion: even 1 coach per 10
  consumer sales lifts blended GP/sale to ~$31.

**Funnel math to beat** (assumptions to validate, not facts):
`$1.50 CPC → 25% run a scan → 8% of scanners buy` = $75 per sale ❌ too high.
The levers, in order: (1) CVR scan→buy (the offer's job — guarantee + stack),
(2) CPC (creative's job — better hooks), (3) scan rate (landing page's job).
Getting to ≤$12.50 CAC realistically requires ~3% visitor→buyer, which is why
the free scan must deliver a "holy shit" moment before any paywall.

## 6. Paid ads playbook

**Platform order:** 1) **Google Search** (highest intent — people literally typing
"ats resume checker"), 2) **Meta** (scale, cheap CPMs, emotional hooks), 3) TikTok
(if Meta creative works, port it).

### Google Search (start here — $30/day)
- Campaign: exact/phrase match on: `ats resume checker`, `resume keyword checker`,
  `resume scanner free`, `jobscan free alternative`, `why am i not getting interviews`
- Ad copy: **H:** "Free ATS Resume Check — 10 Seconds" / "See Why You're Filtered Out"
  **D:** "Your resume never leaves your browser. Match score + missing keywords, free.
  One-time $29 Pro — not another subscription."
- Landing: the app section directly (`/#app`). Intent is do-it-now.

### Meta (add at $50/day once Google proves the funnel)
- **Targeting:** broad, 22–54, interests: job hunting/LinkedIn/Indeed — then let
  the algorithm optimize. Creative does the targeting (Hormozi: the hook IS the filter).
- **Creative #1 (best bet — screen recording, 20s):** resume scores 31 → cut to
  three keyword fixes → rescan scores 84. Caption: "The software rejecting you
  takes 10 seconds to beat."
- **Hooks to rotate (callout + pain):**
  - "Applied to 100 jobs and heard nothing back? It's not you. It's a parser."
  - "A recruiter never saw your resume. Software threw it out first."
  - "75% of resumes are rejected by a robot. Here's yours, scored, free."
  - "Stop paying $50/month to beat a keyword filter."
- **Format:** ugly-native beats polished. Screen recordings and talking-head-style
  captions outperform designed graphics for tool products.
- **Optimization event:** without a Meta pixel (privacy positioning), optimize for
  link clicks first; judge on Gumroad sales per ad-set discount code.

### Measurement without creepy tracking
- **Discount codes per channel:** GOOGLE10, META10, TIKTOK10 (10% off — the
  discount *is* the tracking pixel).
- Optional: add [GoatCounter](https://www.goatcounter.com) (free, no cookies,
  doesn't touch resume text) for page/scan counts. Keeps the privacy claim intact.

### Budget ladder & kill criteria
1. **Days 1–4:** Google $30/day. Kill any keyword with CPC > $3 or 0 scans in 50 clicks.
2. **Days 5–10:** winner keywords to $60/day; start Meta $50/day, 3 creatives × 2 hooks.
3. **Kill rule (Hormozi):** any ad that has spent 1× price ($29) with zero sales dies.
   Any ad at CAC ≤ $12.50 gets budget doubled every 3 days.
4. **Scale rule:** while blended CAC ≤ $12.50 and Gumroad payout > weekly ad spend,
   keep doubling. Stop scaling the moment CAC crosses $20 for 3 consecutive days.
5. **Weekly:** replace the worst 50% of creatives with new hooks. Never touch a winner.

   fix → 84. Text overlay: "free tool, link in bio."
2. "Rating my followers' resumes against real job posts" (duet/react format).
3. "3 words that get your resume auto-rejected" (weak-phrases check content).

## 8. What "Hormozi-approved" still requires from reality

- **Proof.** The stack has zero testimonials because there are zero customers.
  Never fabricate them (illegal + brand-fatal). Collect: Gumroad follow-up email
  asks "did your interview rate change?" — screenshot replies become the proof wall.
- **Price is a hypothesis.** $29 vs $49 should be A/B'd via discount codes once
  there's traffic. The framework says charge more than feels comfortable.
- **The offer beats the ads.** If CAC won't drop below $25, the fix is the offer
  (stronger guarantee, more stack), not more ad spend.
