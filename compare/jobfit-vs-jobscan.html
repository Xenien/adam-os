Jobscan is the best-known name in resume scanning, it's been at this for years, and its Premium product is genuinely feature-rich — LinkedIn optimization, cover letter tools, a job tracker. We built JobFit anyway, because we think the two products answer different questions: theirs is a subscription platform for managing a job search; ours is a $29-once checker for making sure a recruiter's search can find you. When Jobscan is the better fit

Honestly: if you want a full job-search platform — a tracker for dozens of applications, LinkedIn profile optimization, cover letter tooling — and you'll use it hard for a month or two, Jobscan's subscription buys you more surface area than we offer. We're not trying to be that.

When JobFit is the better fit

If what you need is the core job — does the parser read my resume, and will the recruiter's keyword search find me? — you shouldn't need a subscription for it, or an account, or to upload your resume to anyone's cloud. JobFit's full diagnosis is free with no meter, the fixes are $29 exactly once, and the whole thing runs in your browser, so the privacy answer isn't a policy — it's architecture.
